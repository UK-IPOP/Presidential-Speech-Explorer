Today, a bipartisan group of former Mayors and Governors from across the country signed a letter supporting the Bipartisan Infrastructure Framework, citing an "overwhelming need for improvement" and the need for infrastructure that can "support a 21st century economy."
In the letter, the group of former officials noted the frequent deferral and cancellation of infrastructure projects in the past, and commended the framework's commitment to improving infrastructure like roads, bridges, water systems, and transit, as well as expanding broadband and making investments to ensure our resiliency in the face of climate change.
The group also promised to work in bipartisan fashion with the White House and Members of Congress from both sides of the aisle to make the infrastructure proposal a reality.
